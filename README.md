# E-Commerce Sales Data Analysis

## Overview
This project analyzes a real-world e-commerce sales dataset containing 
intentional data quality issues including missing values, inconsistent 
column names, formatting errors, outliers, and data entry mistakes. 
The goal is to clean the data and extract meaningful insights into 
customer behavior, product preferences, and sales trends.

## Tools Used
- Python
- Pandas
- Matplotlib
- Seaborn
- Jupyter Notebook

## Business Questions
1. Which product categories are most popular among customers?
2. Do customers prefer a specific payment method?
3. How do sales evolve over time?

## Key Findings
- Books was the most ordered category at 23.9% of total orders, 
  followed by Home (21.7%), Electronics (20.7%), Sports (18.5%), 
  and Clothing (15.2%)
- Cash on Delivery was the dominant payment method at 33% of 
  transactions, followed by PayPal (24%), Bank Transfer (22%), 
  and Credit Card (21%)
- February 2025 recorded the highest monthly revenue at $19,391, 
  followed by June 2025 ($16,376) and October 2025 ($15,883)
- Sales show short-term fluctuations without a stable long-term 
  growth trend

## Conclusion
Books leads in order volume but the difference across categories 
is narrow, suggesting balanced customer interest across all 
product lines. Cash on Delivery dominates payments but the 
remaining 67% is split across digital methods, indicating an 
opportunity to promote faster payment options. Revenue peaks in 
February and June with no consistent seasonal pattern identified.
